\section{Overview}

The tourism industry is experiencing unprecedented growth, with 1.3 billion international visitors recorded in 2019 and 
reaching 89\% of pre-pandemic levels in the first quarter of 2024 \cite{UNWTO2024a, UNWTO2024b}. This surge in tourism 
has a direct impact on the global carbon footprint, not only through the means of travel but also via 
accommodation-related energy consumption \cite{Becken2001, Scott2008}. Research highlights that the energy consumption 
within the accommodation sector is particularly intensive, with forecasts predicting a doubling by 2040 
\cite{Jos2016, Gossling2015}. Notably, electricity alone accounts for approximately 70\% of the total energy used in 
tourist accommodations \cite{Becken2001, Warren2017}.

Water usage in tourist accommodations also presents significant challenges, particularly given the close relationship 
between water and energy consumption. Water conservation efforts are crucial in regions where water is a scarce resource 
\cite{Becken2017, Lam2016}. Furthermore, extreme droughts, which are increasingly associated with climate change, 
threaten the availability of water resources essential for tourism and accommodation operations \cite{Ziervogel2010}. 
This situation is particularly pressing in water-scarce areas like islands and coastal zones, where tourism drives 
economic growth but simultaneously escalates the demand for already limited resources \cite{Gossling2015}.

As climate change continues to exacerbate water scarcity, it is vital to investigate whether adaptive measures can 
mitigate or exacerbate water inequalities among local populations and tourists \cite{Ziervogel2010}. However, 
influencing guest behavior to promote resource conservation remains a significant challenge, especially given the 
difficulty in monitoring guest consumption patterns in real-time \cite{Juvan2014}.

The rise of peer-to-peer accommodations, particularly through platforms like AirBnB, which boasts over 8 million 
listings worldwide \cite{Airbnb2024a, Airbnb2024b}, presents an alternative model where guests have greater autonomy 
over resource usage. Despite this growing trend, research in this area is limited compared to studies focused on hotels, 
highlighting a gap in understanding the environmental impact of this accommodation type \cite{Becken2016, Warren2017}. 

Interestingly, AirBnB has been identified as promoting a more efficient use of existing resources, positioning itself 
as an environmentally sustainable alternative to traditional hotels. For instance, properties listed on AirBnB are 
reported to use 63\% less energy per guest night in North America and 78\% less in Europe compared to traditional hotels 
\cite{Simon2014}. Additionally, AirBnB home-share properties use 12\% less water per guest night in North America and 
48\% less in Europe \cite{Simon2014}, contrasting sharply with the hotel industry's trend toward decreasing efficiency, 
where energy use per room night has doubled since the 1960s \cite{Prairie2012}.

AirBnB's commitment to sustainability is further demonstrated by the fact that nearly 83\% of its North American hosts 
own at least one energy-efficient appliance \cite{Simon2014}. Moreover, AirBnB's rating system allows for the 
identification of wasteful guests, a feature that is less emphasized in hotels where enforcement of resource 
conservation is minimal \cite{Webb2014}. Additionally, research conducted by AirBnB China in 2018 concluded that Chinese 
tourists are increasingly making environmentally conscious choices in their accommodations, influenced by AirBnB's 
promotion of sustainable listings \cite{Airbnb2018}.

The growth of the "green" consumer market segment, which prioritizes sustainability in their tourism experiences, is 
indicative of a broader shift within the industry \cite{Weaver2005}. Tourism accommodation providers must clearly 
communicate their sustainability initiatives to ensure high levels of guest satisfaction and encourage repeat visits, 
with a particular focus on keeping customers informed about their efforts \cite{Rahman2015, Jones2014}.

By providing clear insights into resource consumption, this project seeks to promote more sustainable practices and 
encourage responsible usage among guests and hosts in shared accommodations made available through online platforms like 
AirBnB.

\section{Problem Definition}

The growing concern over resource consumption in short-term rental accommodations, particularly in self-catering 
settings like those provided by platforms such as AirBnB, has been increasingly highlighted in recent research. One of 
the primary issues is the lack of awareness among guests regarding their real-time consumption of resources such as 
electricity and water during their stays. Studies have shown that when guests are made aware of their daily consumption, 
significant reductions can be achieved. For example, it was demonstrated that there was a 27.4\% reduction in 
electricity consumption and a 21.6\% decrease in water usage when guests were informed about their consumption from 
previous days \cite{Warren2018b}. These findings underscore the potential for behavioral change when feedback mechanisms 
are implemented.

Moreover, the impact of feedback on water usage has been further corroborated by studies \cite{Stewart2013}, which found 
that individuals could reduce their shower time by 27\% when provided with real-time feedback. This suggests that 
immediate access to consumption data not only increases awareness but also actively influences behavior, leading to more 
sustainable resource use. However, the challenge remains that many guests are not provided with this information in a 
timely or accessible manner, particularly in self-catering accommodations where resource usage is often left 
unmonitored. A further study emphasizes this gap, noting that guests' unawareness of their real-time resource use poses 
a significant concern for sustainable tourism \cite{Juvan2014}.

To address this problem, it is crucial to implement intelligent monitoring technologies capable of capturing small 
changes in resource use at high frequencies. High-frequency monitoring is essential to detect and respond to variations 
in consumption patterns effectively \cite{Jos2016}. Such technologies can provide both hosts and 
guests with real-time, high-resolution readings of electricity and water usage, thereby facilitating more informed 
decisions and promoting conservation. Other studies, further highlight the importance of these technologies in enhancing 
both guest and host awareness of resource consumption, leading to more sustainable practices \cite{Warren2018a}.

The benefits of implementing such monitoring systems extend beyond resource conservation. For instance, as extreme 
drought events become more frequent, the availability of water is a growing concern for AirBnB hosts. Hosts are 
increasingly concerned about the potential effects of future water scarcity on their businesses \cite{Prinsloo2019}. 
By adapting to these challenges and reducing water consumption, both hosts and guests can contribute 
to mitigating the effects of water shortages. Additionally, monitoring systems may address issues related to indoor 
comfort, such as guests experiencing poor sleep due to uncomfortable temperatures. By delivering real-time data on 
indoor climate conditions, these systems can improve comfort levels and enhance the guest experience 
\cite{Pallesen2016}.

In conclusion, the integration of intelligent monitoring technologies in short-term rental accommodations presents a 
promising solution to the challenges of resource consumption. By making guests and hosts aware of their real-time usage, 
significant reductions in electricity and water consumption can be achieved, contributing to more sustainable and 
resilient tourism practices.

\section{Background Research}

\subsection{Shared Economy Systems}

In recent years, the concept of the shared economy has gained significant traction, offering innovative solutions for 
the optimization of underutilized resources. Shared economy systems are defined as “a socioeconomic system that enables 
an intermediated set of exchanges of goods and services between individuals/organizations which aim to increase 
efficiency and optimization of underutilized resources in society” \cite{Munoz2017}. This approach has been increasingly 
adopted across various sectors, notably in the realm of accommodation services, where platforms like AirBnB have 
revolutionized traditional models of lodging.

AirBnB, founded in 2008 by Brian Chesky, Joe Gebbia, and Nathan Blecharczyk, exemplifies the potential of shared economy 
platforms. What began as a simple initiative to rent out an air mattress in a living room has transformed into a global 
phenomenon with over 4 million listings in 191 countries. This growth reflects not only the financial success of the 
company, valued at over 100 billion dollars, but also its profound impact on the way people travel, stay, and experience 
new places \cite{Nadkarni2020}. The platform has effectively harnessed the principles of the shared economy, offering a 
decentralized alternative to traditional accommodation services.

Significantly, sharing economies such as AirBnB also present opportunities for promoting sustainability. As highlighted 
by \cite{Webb2014}, the model of home-sharing accommodation can contribute to a more sustainable use of resources by 
reducing the environmental footprint associated with travel. AirBnB's potential to foster sustainable practices is 
further exemplified by its introduction of the Sustainable Hosting Plan. This initiative includes a £1 million fund in 
the United Kingdom aimed at helping hosts improve energy efficiency, reduce carbon emissions, and manage rising living 
costs. Through collaboration with the Energy Saving Trust \cite{EST}, AirBnB provides hosts with personalized home 
energy efficiency checks and action plans, connecting them with local installers and offering grants ranging from £1,000 
to £3,000 for improvements. Furthermore, AirBnB's commitment to becoming a net zero company by 2030 underscores 
the platform's ongoing efforts to align with global sustainability goals \cite{Airbnb2022}.

In conclusion, AirBnB not only serves as a successful model of shared economy but also demonstrates the potential for 
such platforms to contribute to sustainability. 

\subsection{Technology Applications in Self-Catering Accomodation}

In the realm of shared economy systems, particularly in the context of short-term rental platforms like AirBnB, the 
integration of smart home devices has become increasingly prevalent. The primary motivations for hosts to adopt these 
technologies include enhanced physical security, improved monitoring capabilities, remote control functionality, and 
overall convenience for guests, as well as the potential for significant monetary benefits \cite{Dey2020}. These 
devices, such as smart thermostats and smart lights, have proven to be instrumental in allowing hosts to monitor and 
manage the energy consumption of their properties effectively. As noted by Dey et al., hosts were keen to implement 
strategies that would enable them to conserve energy, as this directly correlated with reducing operational costs 
associated with their rental properties \cite{Dey2020}.

Moreover, since renting out their property represents a monetary investment, hosts exhibited a strong inclination toward 
using smart home devices to optimize energy efficiency. This was not only to maintain low operational costs but also to 
ensure that their properties remained competitive in the market \cite{Dey2020}. However, despite these advantages, the 
current offerings of smart home technologies do not fully address the unique needs of AirBnB hosts. Specifically, hosts 
expressed a desire for applications and customizations tailored to their distinct circumstances, highlighting a gap in 
the existing technology solutions \cite{Dey2020}.

On the other hand, from the guests' perspective, the presence of smart devices in AirBnB listings generally enhanced 
their experience, with many perceiving these technologies as luxurious additions to their stay \cite{Mare2020}. 
Nevertheless, this positive perception was tempered by concerns regarding privacy and control. Some guests were 
apprehensive about the potential for smart devices to facilitate excessive monitoring by hosts, which could manifest in 
actions such as remotely locking thermostats or other intrusive behaviors \cite{Mare2020}. This dichotomy underscores a 
critical challenge in the adoption of smart technologies within shared economy systems: the need to balance the 
operational benefits for hosts with the privacy and comfort expectations of guests.

In summary, while smart home devices offer significant benefits in the management and operation of AirBnB properties, 
there remain unmet needs and concerns that must be addressed to fully realize their potential in this context. Future 
developments in this area should focus on creating more customized and flexible solutions that cater specifically to the 
needs of both hosts and guests, ensuring that the adoption of such technologies enhances rather than detracts from the 
overall shared economy experience.

\subsection{Smart Monitoring and the Internet of Things}

Smart home technology has evolved significantly since its inception in the 1970s with the creation of the X10 protocol, 
which facilitated control of lights and appliances over existing electrical wiring \cite{Caflisch2023}. Despite its 
initial limitations in functionality and reliability, the advent of the internet in the 1990s marked a pivotal 
development in the evolution of smart home systems. This innovation allowed users to interact with their homes through 
web interfaces and mobile applications, thereby enhancing the utility and appeal of smart home technologies.

The integration of real-time monitoring capabilities further transformed the landscape of smart home systems. Users 
could now track energy, water, and gas usage with unprecedented precision, which attracted individuals aiming to reduce 
utility bills. Smart home technology has increasingly focused on energy efficiency and sustainability, with contemporary 
devices designed to optimize energy consumption and minimize environmental impact \cite{Caflisch2023}. These 
advancements enable users to set energy goals for specific devices or entire homes, with measurements available in terms 
of days, dollars, or watts. Additionally, individual sensors can be added to monitor large energy consumers or pool 
pumps more accurately.

This shift towards real-time feedback is grounded in the objective of enhancing energy literacy and fostering behavioral 
changes that lead to lower electricity consumption \cite{Webb2014}. For instance, a home energy monitor company, Sense, 
reports that its average users save approximately 9\% on their electric bills \cite{Walker2023}.

\subsection{Smart Monitoring in the Hospitality Industry}

Despite the substantial advancements in smart monitoring technology, empirical research on the deployment of Internet of 
Things (IoT) solutions in the hospitality industry remains limited. While the concept of IoT has been around for nearly 
a decade \cite{Atzori2010}, its application in hospitality, particularly for data generation and optimization, is a more 
recent phenomenon.

The environmental impact of the hospitality industry is multifaceted, encompassing CO2 emissions, food demand, water and 
electricity usage, waste disposal, construction activity, and land use. Various IoT applications are emerging to address 
these issues, with an estimated potential to achieve savings of over 10 percent \cite{Kratzert2020}. For instance, 
Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) systems facilitate two-way communication between utilities and customers, 
enabling near real-time monitoring of energy and water consumption. AMI systems offer numerous benefits, including 
accurate billing, reduced operational costs, and features such as leakage detection \cite{Monks2019}.

Energy management has emerged as a leading area for IoT investment \cite{Naimat2017}, which is of particular relevance 
to the hospitality industry due to its resource-intensive operations and often constrained environments 
\cite{Styles2014}. However, the potential for IoT in water conservation remains largely underutilized, despite the 
increasing prevalence of water-stressed destinations \cite{Tortella2011}. Notably, luxury hotel guests consume between 
200 and 1,000 liters of water per day, significantly exceeding the consumption levels of the local population 
\cite{Becken2014}.

Moreover, a significant portion of data generated by IoT remains untapped, highlighting a substantial opportunity for 
further utilization \cite{Manyika2015}. The interaction between IoT and Big Data underscores the potential for a 
transformative shift in enterprise architecture and decision-making processes within the hospitality industry 
\cite{Nadkarni2020}.

According to \cite{Zaslavsky2013}, while IoT generates data characterized by high volumes, variety, and velocity, 
traditional computing frameworks are likely to become limiting factors. They argue that these frameworks must be 
augmented with cloud-based infrastructure capable of adapting to fluctuating data loads to provide the necessary 
elasticity. The connection between IoT and Big Data necessitates advancements in speed and storage capacities to 
accommodate the rapid growth of data generated by "things."

In summary, smart home technology and IoT have revolutionized utility management, offering significant benefits in 
energy and water efficiency. Although these technologies are well-developed, their application in the hospitality 
industry is still emerging. Addressing challenges such as data underutilization and adapting computing frameworks will 
be crucial to maximizing IoT's potential, paving the way for more sustainable practices in the industry.

\subsection{Research Need and Knowledge Gap}

The growing importance of shared economy systems necessitates the adaptation of their ecosystems to enhance transparency 
and trust among stakeholders. According to \cite{Deng2021}, improving transparency for both guests and hosts is crucial 
for fostering trust within the shared economy. This is particularly relevant in platforms like AirBnB, where the 
interaction between guests and hosts is fundamental to the service provided.

In this context, there is a pressing need to develop intelligent technology platforms that empower providers and users 
of shared services to make informed and trustworthy decisions \cite{Hamari2016}. Such platforms can leverage data to 
enhance the visibility of critical information and facilitate more transparent interactions. Currently, a significant 
gap exists in the visibility of data collected by smart devices during AirBnB stays. \cite{Mare2020} highlights that 
guests lack access to the information gathered about them, which underscores the necessity for systems that provide 
greater insight into data collection practices.

Furthermore, the scarcity of local official statistics at the district level regarding tourists in shared accommodations 
exacerbates the challenge of understanding and managing tourism trends \cite{Johns2007}. This lack of granular data 
limits the ability of policymakers and stakeholders to make informed decisions about the shared economy sector. In 
response to this challenge, the United Kingdom's Office for National Statistics (ONS) recognized in 2022 the need for 
novel data sources to enhance tourism statistics. The ONS advocated for the integration of data from booking portals, 
mobile phones, payment cards, and travel agencies to better understand tourism dynamics \cite{ONS2022}. This approach 
underscores the potential for advanced data collection methods to provide more comprehensive insights into the shared 
economy and improve decision-making processes.

In conclusion, the evolution of shared economy systems hinges on enhancing transparency and developing intelligent 
technology platforms that facilitate informed decision-making. Addressing the current lack of data visibility for guests 
is essential for improving trust and understanding within this sector. By integrating novel data sources and advancing 
data collection methods, stakeholders can gain valuable insights and better manage tourism dynamics, ultimately 
contributing to a more transparent and effective shared economy ecosystem.

\section{Aim and Objectives}

The main aim of this project is to develop an integrated smart monitoring and reporting system for AirBnB properties 
that enhances resource efficiency and environmental sustainability by providing real-time data on energy consumption, 
water usage, and temperature. This system aims to raise awareness among guests and hosts about their resource use, 
foster responsible behavior, and support the broader goal of sustainable tourism.

\subsubsection{Objectives}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Develop and deploy smart sensors to monitor energy consumption, water usage, and indoor temperature within 
    AirBnB properties. Configure the system to enable real-time data collection and transmission.
    \item Build an Application Programming Interface (API) to facilitate access to sensor-collected data for 
    third-party developers and researchers. Ensure the API follows the OpenAPI principles.
    \item Design and generate comprehensive daily reports for guests detailing their resource usage. Provide a final 
    report at the end of the stay summarizing total consumption and offer insights on environmental impact.
    \item Create a user-friendly web application that allows hosts to monitor and manage their property's resource 
    consumption effectively. Include features for viewing historical data, and generating reports.
\end{itemize}
