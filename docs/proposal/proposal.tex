\section*{Summary}
\end{center}

The proposal aims to develop a comprehensive solution to reduce the carbon impact of staying at an Airbnb, with the goal of promoting environmental consciousness 
among tourists and hosts. The project will leverage data analytics to collect and analyze resource consumption metrics including temperature, energy and water, providing 
tourists with personalized resource usage information of their stay. This platform will also enable hosts to monitor and optimize resource usage, offering insights into energy 
and water expenditure on a daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly basis. 

Additionally, predictive analytics will be employed to forecast resource consumption and potential revenue for future Airbnb investments, encouraging hosts to adopt eco-friendly 
practices. The ultimate aim is to expand the network of environmentally conscious Airbnb hosts and tourists through awareness campaigns and incentive programs, fostering a culture of 
sustainability within the hospitality industry.

In 2021 me, my wife and our two young daugters moved to a 35m2 tiny house in the tropical forest of the Central Pacific of Costa Rica. This was our all time dream, and our final step on
achieving our goal of living the most sustainable life possible at the moment. My wife, an Environmental Engineer and a Sustainable Urban Designer included the use of a
natural water treatment system, a dry toilet, small low energy consumption appliances and correct placement of the house for efficient light and breeze harvesting into the design from
an early stage. It was built keeping construction waste to a minimum, with durable long lasting materials and by local workers. 

A year later we finished a second tiny house, this time with a slighty different design (we added air conditioning because of summer temperatures), but with the same principles. 
Our goal was to spread this lifestyle to other people by renting it on Airbnb and get travelers to experience a different way of living. We use biodegradable cleaning products and 
encourage them to recycle, compost and save energy and water. Although we had really good experiences with our guests and great feedback, we noticed an increase in our utility bills.

That's when we thought of monitoring the resources consumption of our guests and compare it with others. We also started to look for ways to share the data with them, keep a detailed 
record of our cost of service and last to find a way for Airbnb to reward them for their sustainable practices. We found that there was no solution for this, so we decided to create one.

\subsection{Overview}

\subsection{Problem Definition (Presentation of the Problem)}

Currently there are no solutions to keep track of the amount of resources (water and electricity) used by Airbnb guests. Even if there are ways to obtain these values, there are no solutions 
to gather this data, process it, analyze it, compare it with other data and share it with guests, hosts and Airbnb. This is due to modern monitoring systems keeping their data private and 
not being interoperable with other solutions. Also there has not been an initiative from Airbnb to create something like this.

\subsection{Literary Review (Background Research)}

\subsubsection{Airbed and Breakfast}

Airbed and Breakfast or Airbnb was founded in 2008 by Brian Chesky, Joe Gebbia, and Nathan Blecharczyk. The idea was to rent out an air mattress in their living room to help pay their 
rent. It has since grown to be a global platform with over 4 million listings in 191 countries. The company has been valued at over 100 billion dollars and 
revolutionized the way people travel, stay, and experience new places \cite{Satya2023}.

\subsubsection{Why Airbnb?}

Airbnb was chosen for this project because it is a platform that has grown exponentially in Costa Rica in the last decade and has become one of the most popular choice for travelers 
\cite{Neale2024}. Also an Airbnb is own by the creator of this project, which will make it easier to test the system in a real environment, but the idea was to create a solution that can 
be used by any kind  of accommodation.

\subsubsection{Tourists looking for small footprint destinations}

The idea of offering sustainable alternatives to travelers comes from the fact that they are actually looking for these types of accommodations. In a recent study (2023) The World 
Economic Forum found that 80\% of travelers want more sustainable options when traveling \cite{SustainableTravelReport2023}. Some other interesting facts about this study are 
\cite{SustainableTravelReport2023}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 49\% would like some type of discount of economic incentive for choosing a sustainable option
    \item 42\% would be more encouraged to stay on these places if there was a reward program to excange points for discounts or some other perks
    \item 67\% of travelers are turning they air conditioning off when they leave the room
    \item 77\% of travelers are turning off the lights when they are not using them
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Smart home technology}

Smart home technology as we know it today started back in the 1970's with the creation of the X10 protocol\cite{Caflisch2023}. Lights and appliances were controlled over existing 
electrical wiring, but with limited and unreliable functionality. In the next decade the technology matured but it was not until the 1990's, with the creation of the internet that 
these systems started to be more useful. Users were now able to interact with their homes through web interfaces or mobile apps.

This also allowed monitoring the usage of energy, water and gas in real-time which attracted people with an interest in reducing their utilities bills. In fact \enquote{smart home technology 
has increasingly focused on energy efficiency and sustainability, with devices designed to optimize energy usage and reduce environmental impact} \cite{Caflisch2023}. Now people can 
set energy goals for individual devices or your entire home, measured by day, dollars, or watts. Additionally, you can add individual sensors to large energy consumers or pool pumps 
for more precise monitoring. For time-of-use plans, you can input your local rate zones to obtain more accurate billing information \cite{SanSone2022}.

The idea behind this is to enhance energy literacy and encourage behavior changes that lower electricity consumption by offering instant feedback on electricity usage \cite{Webb2014}. One 
home energy monitor company, Sense, estimates that their average user saves about 9\% on electric bills \cite{Walker2023}.

\subsubsection{Monitoring of Airbnb rental homes for resources usage}

There are not many studies or articles on monitoring rental homes listed on Airbnb at this moment. The best one found was in fact with the purpose of figuring out if guests were less 
energy efficient than their hosts. This study used the monthly reports from an electricity company to compare usage between guests and hosts, which was almost three times as high for 
guests \cite{Halford2023}. This coincides with the fact that overall renters are more likely to spend more energy, water and gas than homeowners \cite{Jeunesse2013}. It also states 
that: \enquote{The gap in energy usage between owners and renters suggests that there are real opportunities for savings through some combination of added incentives for property owners to 
make these investments in retrofits and greater incentives for tenants to conserve energy} \cite{Jeunesse2013}.

\subsubsection{Is Airbnb more ecofriendly than hotels?}

There is a big debate on this and it really depends on who's conducting the study and what is taken into account. Some of the studies include the type of transportation used and even 
how the money earned from Airbnb is spent \cite{Power2021}. A study by Airbnb and Cleantech Group revealed that travelers staying in Airbnb properties consume significantly less energy 
than traditional hotel guests \cite{Simon2014}. In North America, Airbnb guests use 63 percent less energy and, over a single year, saved enough water to fill 270 Olympic-sized pools
 \cite{Simon2014}. Additionally, nearly 83 percent of Airbnb hosts in North America own at least one energy-efficient appliance, reflecting a strong commitment to sustainable practices
 \cite{Simon2014}. This constrasts with the reality of the hotel industry, where the trend toward less and less efficient hotels is clear, with energy use per room-night doubling between 
 the 1960s and the 2000s \cite{Prairie2012}.

\subsubsection{Airbnb's environmental awareness}

Airbnb introduced its Sustainable Hosting Plan, which includes a £1 million fund to help hosts improve energy efficiency, reduce carbon emissions, and save on their bills, 
addressing the rising cost of living. Partnering with Energy Saving Trust, Airbnb will offer all Hosts a personalized home energy efficiency check and action plan, detailing steps to 
enhance their home's efficiency. Hosts interested in making improvements will be connected to local installers and can receive grants of £1,000 or £3,000. Additionally, last year, 
Airbnb set a goal to become a net zero company by 2030 \cite{Airbnb2022}.

\subsection{Aims and Objectives}

The main aim of this project is to develop a data-driven solution that educates tourists about the carbon footprint of Airbnb stays, empowers hosts to optimize resource usage, and 
promotes sustainable practices in the hospitality industry.

\subsubsection{Objectives}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Create a robust system to collect data on room temperature, water consumption, and energy usage in Airbnb accommodations.
    \item Process, analize and compare data in a way that can be used to reduce the carbon footprint of Airbnb stays.
    \item Calculate an average score for resources usage per guest per day.
    \item Generate a report for tourists with personalized resource usage information.
    \item Develop an intuitive interface for hosts to monitor and optimize resource usage.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Device layer}

\subsubsection{Hardware}

It consists of three microcontrollers, each controls a sensor. One measures water consumption, the other energy usage and the third one indoor and outdoor temperatures. 
These microcontrollers are connected to a WiFi access point.

The idea is to capture how much of these resources (water and energy) are used by every guest. These sensors give real time data that is then used to calculate the total amount. Temperature 
readings are used to compare the difference between the outside and inside to determine if people are trying to keep a very low temperature even if they don't have to.

\subsubsection{Software}

Open source Zephyr Project (RTOS) runs on top of these microcontrollers to allow a secure and resource aware solution for these embedded systems. This was chosen as opposed to running
the code on top of the chip because it is a software platform already tested by a lot of projects and that supports internet connection, sensors, over-the-air updates and an active 
community.

\subsection{Data layer}

Two separate databases are kept, a time series database for every reading from the sensors and a non-relational database for user, house, device data and metadata. For the time series 
database the open source ThingsBoard IoT Platform was used because it has integrated data collection, processing, visualization, and device management. The idea was to capture time 
series data on this platform and then use its own REST API to connect to the application which in the end will run tha main program. 

\subsection{Communication layer}

It is basically a REST API that connects the Data layer with the application. It works as a way for the application to have access to user, home, device (including time series) data. 
It complies with Open API specification for standardization. This layer allows clients (applications) to access data in a secure and standard way.

\subsection{Presentation layer}

A web application that can be accessed from personal computers, tablets and cellphones. It has two modes: host and traveler, the host mode will give access to the Airbnb host to in 
depth real time data of every listing with historical data on resources consumption by travelers. The traveler mode has a simpler version where an interactive report of their stay 
can be seen. This is the main program that runs the solution and how users interact with their data.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Install energy, water and temperature sensors on a real Airbnb
    \item Gather information from the sensors and store it on a time series database (locally or on the cloud)
    \item Process data remotely to calculate watts/hour and liters consumed during stay
    \item Calculate difference between inside temperature, outside temperature and optimal environment temperature during stay
    \item Compare figures from 3 with data from hotel stays
    \item Generate a report for guest with scores and detailed results and ways to improve
    \item Present real-time data and calculations to host with user friendly dashboard
    \item Generate report for host with insights on spendings and tips to increase savings
\end{enumerate}
